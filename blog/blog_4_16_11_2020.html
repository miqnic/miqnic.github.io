Creating Graphs for my Data

My research is all about the effect of a person’s self-perception to how other people’s care for them and their grades in various subjects. This makes the self-perception our independent or explanatory variable while the subject grades will be the dependent or response variable. Below are the graphs and code for the following:


This is the univariate graph of the SUPPORTSUM variable. The graph is unimodal with its highest peak at the last category. The first two categories only have a few results when compared to the last two. The graph is skewed to the left as there are higher frequencies in higher categories.



The one above is the univariate graph of the GRADEAVE variable. The graph is unimodal with its highest peak at the last category. Unlike the first graph, all categories have values that are somehow spread out of all of them. The graph is also skewed to the left as there are higher frequencies in higher categories.

Both of these are categorical ones so following the flowchart that was given, I have to collapse the response variable into two categories only. Since I have only four valid values to consider in the grades, I would collapse them into grades that have 50% and above averages and those that did not.


I then mapped these categories into words that would be readable to the end user. The code is found below:


With these in mind, the output graph of these two variables will result into a category to category bar graph with self-perception categories in the X-axis and the grades in the Y-axis.



The graph shows that the respondents who felt like people supported them in a very little amount has garnered the highest grade average followed by those who somewhat felt supported. Those who are supported quite a bit were next in line while those who are not at all supported showed the lowest grade average out of all the support categories.
